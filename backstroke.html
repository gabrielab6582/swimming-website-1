Learn to Swim Backstroke

Backstroke (or back crawl) is, as its name implies, the only one of the four competitive swimming strokes swum on the back.


Step 1 Flutter kick



Start on your back with a kick board and your arms out over your head.

When your body aligned straight begin to kick a flutter kick, like the kick for freestyle but on your back.




Step 2: Arms, Arms, Arms!



hen your arm hits the water, bring it down and scull outward to propel yourself forward. As you do this, raise the other arm and perform the same motion. Repeat. This should feel natural. Try to keep a steady rhythm between your arms and legs because that will make your swimming faster and easier!


Step 3: Breathing


A good policy is to breathe in as one arm leaves the water, then breathe out as the other leaves the water




Step 4: Put It All Together



Now with your arms, leg and breath put the motions together.

Just like freestyle, patience is key when swimming. So do not get discouraged when you sink down or cannot propel forward. With lots of practice sooner or later you'll be swimming freely.
