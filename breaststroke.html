Learn to Swim Breaststroke


Step 1 Initial Position


In the initial position, you float in a horizontal position on your chest.


Your arms are held together and extended forward, palms facing down. Your head is in line with your trunk, and you look down. Your legs are held together, and your feet are pointed.




Step 2: Action Phase of the Arms




From the initial postion your palms turn outwards, your arms move apart and your body moves into a Y-like position.


When your arms are just outside of your shoulders, your elbows point upwards and your hands continue to move back and downwards.


Once your hands have moved past your shoulders, they move back and inward, until they meet in front of your chest (insweep)



Step 3: Kick



As your hands move towards each other, your head and shoulders rise above the water, your knees flex and your pointed feet begin to recover back toward your butt.


Then your feet kick back and apart



Step 4: Put It All Together



As your hands move towards each other, your head and shoulders rise above the water. Your knees flex, and your feet start to recover towards your butt.

Your upper body at this point is at this highest when your hands meet in front of your chest. Your arms start to move forward immediately for the recovery, while your feet continue to move towards your butt.


As your arms recover forward, your legs end their recovery with the feet being close to the butt.


Then the propulsive phase of the legs begins. Your feet kick back and apart while your arms extend completely under water, and your chest and head drop back into the water.
