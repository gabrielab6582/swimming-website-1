Learn to Swim Freestyle

Freestyle is one of the most known swimming strokes.


The freestyle stroke, also known as front crawl, is the fastest and most efficient of the competitive swimming strokes.


Step 1: Kick the Legs



Sit at the ledge of the pool with legs straight in front of you


Begin to kick the legs from your hips. This kick is known as a flutter kick. Avoid large bend in the knees when kicking


After becoming comfortable with the kicking, grab a kick board, and hop into the water


Holding onto the kick board directly in front of you, push off the wall with both feet and kick the same way as on the pool ledge.



Step 2: Move Those Arms


Standing outside of the pool. Put your arms in front of you and begin to move them in circular motions


Next, move into the water and grab your kick board. Lie down on your stomach in the water (floating!) with their arms extended with the board. Pull one arm underwater to your hip.


Then lift the arm that was pulled back out of the water and put it back on the board where it started. Move on the other arm and then the other.


Step 3: Now Breath


The final step before you can start swimming is understanding the breathing technique



Naturally humans breathe in and out through their nose. This is not the case when swimming. Take a breath before entering your head into the water.


When your face is in the water and looking down to the bottom of the pool blow out air through your nose.


This should create a continuous stream of bubbles.


As you rotate your head to the side, without lifting your neck out of the water, take a breath through your mouth and rotate your head back into the water.



Step 4: Put It All Together




Now with your arms, leg and breath put the motions together.

Once you have a continuous rhythm going with the kick board, put the board down and begin practicing the same steps.

Patience is key when swimming. So do not get discouraged when you sink down or cannot propel forward. With lots of practice sooner or later you'll be swimming freely.
